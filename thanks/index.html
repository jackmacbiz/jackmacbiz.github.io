The Prop Firm Pass Playbook

Why 90% blow the challenge — and the exact process that passes it

A no-fluff system for passing funded-trader evaluations (Topstep, Apex, MFF, FTMO-style) without gambling your reset fee. Built from what actually separates passers from the 90% who fail.

The real reason you're failing (it's not your strategy)

Prop firms don't make money on payouts — they make it on reset fees. The eval is engineered around the two things retail traders can't control: daily loss limits and consistency rules. You don't fail because your setup is bad. You fail because one tilt-trade breaches the daily loss limit, or you hit the target so erratically the consistency rule voids it.

Pass rate isn't a strategy problem. It's a risk-architecture problem.

The 4 numbers that decide everything

Before a single trade, write these on a sticky note:

1. Max daily loss → divide by 4. That's your hard stop for the day. Hit it, you're done. No exceptions.

2. Per-trade risk → max 0.5% of account (not 1–2%). At 0.5%, it takes 8 consecutive losers to hit your daily stop. You won't lose 8 in a row if you stop at 2.

3. Daily target → 0.5× the per-day pace needed to hit the profit goal in 20 trading days. Slower is safer; the clock is longer than your discipline.

4. Consistency cap → no single day > 30–40% of total profit (most firms void payouts otherwise). Spread it out.

The pass process (run this every session)

  • Trade only the first 90 minutes of your session's prime window (NY open for NQ/ES). Most blow-ups happen in low-quality afternoon chop.
  • Max 3 trades/day. After 2 losers, you're done — period. After 1 winner that hits your daily target, you're done — bank it.
  • One setup. Pick the single highest-probability pattern you actually have data on. The eval is not where you experiment.
  • Pre-define exit before entry. Stop and target set on the order, not in your head.

    • The psychology rule that does the heavy lifting

    You will be tempted to "make it back" after a loss. That single impulse fails more evals than any market move. The fix is mechanical, not emotional: after a losing trade, you are not allowed to re-enter for 15 minutes. Set a timer. This one rule, followed, passes evals.

    Track it or repeat it

    You cannot fix what you don't measure. Log every eval trade: entry, exit, R, the rule you followed or broke. The pattern that passes you is hiding in your own data — most traders never look. (A trade-sync tool like TradeSyncer automates this; a spreadsheet works too. The point is you *review*.)

    The 20-day pass plan

  • Days 1–15: hit 0.5× daily pace, never breach daily stop. You'll likely be ~75% to target with zero stress.
  • Days 16–20: you only need small green days. Reduce size, protect the cushion.
  • The traders who pass are *boring* by day 18. Be boring.
